How it works

You talk. The agents build.

Vybe lives where you already work — in Claude Code, inside your own project. You describe what you want in plain language; Vybe's agents handle the machinery. You stay in control the whole way.

1

Point Vybe at your app

In your project directory, tell Claude Code to port your app. It reads your codebase and interviews you about what the app does and needs.

~/your-app $ port app to vybe
2

Agree on the plan

Vybe proposes how it'll take you to production: architecture, services, data. Nothing happens until you approve.

3

Vybe builds it

The agents provision everything below the waterline — hosting, CI/CD, auth, database, monitoring, security. Your app on production infrastructure in a couple of hours.

4

Validate & accept

You review the ported app running on the new stack and accept or decline. Nothing's final until you're satisfied.

5

Get your onboarding kit

A complete kit lands in your inbox: access to your git repo and accounts, plus instructions you can load straight into Claude Code.

6

Ship by talking

Found a bug? Tell Claude Code what's wrong and it fixes the code. Test locally, then deploy. Iterate as often as you like.

~/your-app $ deploy
7

Always yours

It all runs in your own cloud account, documented and separable. The day you want to take it in-house, you take it.
